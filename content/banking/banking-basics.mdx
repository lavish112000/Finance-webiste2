## How Banks Actually Work: The Business Model

### The Core Function: Intermediation

**Banks are middlemen** between savers (you) and borrowers (businesses, homebuyers).

**The Profit Mechanism:**

1. **Collect deposits** from you at **3-4% interest** (savings account, FD)
2. **Lend that money** to borrowers at **8-12% interest** (home loans, personal loans, business loans)
3. **Keep the spread** as profit (5-8 percentage points = Net Interest Margin or NIM)

**Example (Simplified):**

| Activity | Amount | Interest Rate | Interest Earned/Paid |
|----------|--------|---------------|----------------------|
| Your FD deposit | ₹1,00,000 | 7% | Bank pays you ₹7,000/year |
| Bank lends to homebuyer | ₹1,00,000 | 9% | Bank earns ₹9,000/year |
| **Bank's profit** | - | - | **₹2,000/year** (NIM) |

**Reality check:**
- Bank must keep **CRR (4%)** with RBI (earns nothing)
- Bank must keep **SLR (18%)** in government bonds (earns 6-7%)
- Only **75-78%** of deposits lent out
- Actual NIM: 3-4% (not 2% above, due to regulatory requirements + defaults)

---

### How Banks Make Money: 4 Revenue Streams

**1. Net Interest Income (65-75% of revenue)**

The core business:
- **Interest earned** on loans (home, auto, personal, business)
- **Minus** interest paid on deposits (savings, FD)
- **= Net Interest Income (NII)**

**Example (HDFC Bank FY24)**:
- Interest earned: ₹2,10,000 Cr
- Interest paid: ₹1,20,000 Cr
- **NII: ₹90,000 Cr**

---

**2. Fee Income (20-25% of revenue)**

**No capital required**, pure profit:
- Account maintenance fees (₹500-1,000/year for zero balance violations)
- ATM usage fees (beyond free limit, ₹20-25 per transaction)
- Debit/credit card annual fees (₹500-5,000 depending on card)
- NEFT/RTGS fees (₹2-25 per transaction, though reducing)
- Loan processing fees (0.5-2% of loan amount)
- Forex markup (2-3.5% on international transactions)
- SMS/email alerts (₹25-50/month)

**High-margin business**—why banks push credit cards, wealth management

---

**3. Treasury Income (5-10% of revenue)**

Banks trade in:
- Government bonds (G-Secs)
- Corporate bonds
- Foreign exchange
- Derivatives

**Volatile income** (can swing positive/negative based on interest rate movements, currency fluctuations)

---

**4. Third-Party Product Distribution (3-5% of revenue)**

Banks earn commissions selling:
- Mutual funds (0.5-1% commission)
- Insurance (10-35% of first-year premium)
- Demat account opening (referral fees)
- Gold coins, forex cards

### Regulatory Framework: RBI's Role

**Reserve Bank of India (RBI)** = Banking system regulator + central bank

**Key Controls:**

| Tool | Current Rate | Purpose |
|------|--------------|---------|
| **Repo Rate** | 6.50% | Cost at which banks borrow from RBI (controls lending rates) |
| **CRR** | 4.00% | % of deposits banks must park with RBI (no interest, controls liquidity) |
| **SLR** | 18.00% | % of deposits banks must invest in government bonds (ensures safety) |

**Why this matters to you:**

- **Repo rate rises** (RBI fighting inflation) → Bank FD rates rise (good for savers) + Loan EMIs rise (bad for borrowers)
- **Repo rate falls** (RBI supporting growth) → FD rates fall + EMIs fall

**Banking Licenses:**

| Type | Examples | Allowed Activities |
|------|----------|-------------------|
| **Scheduled Commercial Bank** | HDFC, SBI, ICICI | Full banking (deposits, loans, forex, everything) |
| **Small Finance Bank** | AU, Ujjivan, Equitas | Focus on underserved (priority sector), limited forex |
| **Payments Bank** | Paytm, Airtel, India Post | Deposits up to ₹2L, no lending (only remittances, cards) |
| **Cooperative Bank** | Urban/rural co-ops | Local focus, limited RBI oversight (higher risk) |

## Types of Bank Accounts in India

### 1. Savings Account (Most Common)

**Purpose**: Day-to-day transactions + emergency fund storage

**Features:**

| Feature | Typical Value | Notes |
|---------|---------------|-------|
| **Interest Rate** | 2.70% - 4.00% p.a. | Calculated daily, credited quarterly |
| **Minimum Balance** | ₹0 - ₹10,000 | PSU banks: ₹3,000-5,000; Private: ₹10,000-25,000 (metros) |
| **Withdrawals** | Unlimited | But only 4-5 free ATM transactions/month (other banks) |
| **Liquidity** | Instant | Access via debit card, UPI, net banking 24×7 |
| **Tax** | TDS if interest > ₹10,000/year (₹50,000 for senior citizens) | Fully taxable as "other income" |

**Best For:**
- ✅ Emergency fund (3-6 months expenses)
- ✅ Salary credit
- ✅ Daily expenses
- ❌ Long-term savings (inflation at 5-6% > interest 3-4% = **real return negative**)

**Interest Calculation Example:**

**Month 1**: Average balance ₹50,000 × 3.5% p.a. = ₹145/month
**Month 2**: Average balance ₹80,000 × 3.5% p.a. = ₹233/month
**Quarterly credit**: ₹(145+145+233) = ₹523

**When to choose FD:**

| Your Goal | FD Suitable? | Reason |
|-----------|--------------|--------|
| Emergency fund (3-6 months) | ❌ | Use savings account (need instant access) |
| Short-term goal (6 months-2 years) | ✅ | Better than savings, no stock market risk |

### 4. Recurring Deposit (RD) — Systematic Savings

**Purpose**: Save fixed amount every month, earn FD-like interest

**How It Works:**

- Deposit **fixed amount monthly** (e.g., ₹5,000/month)
- Lock-in for **fixed tenure** (1-5 years)
- Earn **FD-equivalent interest** (6-7.5% p.a.)
- Lump sum returned at maturity (principal + compound interest)

**Example (₹5,000/month for 3 years @ 7% p.a.):**

- Total invested: ₹5,000 × 36 months = ₹1,80,000
- Interest earned: ~₹19,500
- **Maturity value: ₹1,99,500**

**RD vs SIP (Mutual Fund)**:

| Feature | RD | SIP (Equity MF) |
|---------|----|----|
| Returns | 6-7% guaranteed | 12-15% expected (not guaranteed) |
| Risk | Zero | Market risk (can lose money) |
| Tenure | Fixed lock-in | Can stop anytime |
| Best For | Risk-averse, short-term (1-3 years) | Long-term wealth (5+ years), higher risk appetite |

**Our take**: For goals >5 years, SIP in index funds historically beats RD (but requires discipline during market falls)

## Digital Banking Revolution: UPI & Beyond

### UPI (Unified Payments Interface) — India's Global Payment Success Story

**What is UPI:**

Real-time payment system connecting 350+ banks via single mobile app

**How it works:**

1. Link bank account to UPI app (GPay, PhonePe, Paytm, BHIM)
2. Create **UPI ID** (e.g., yourname@oksbi, 9876543210@paytm)
3. Send/receive money **instantly** using UPI ID or phone number (no account number/IFSC needed)
4. Authenticate with **UPI PIN** (4-6 digit, set by you)

---

| Year | Monthly Transactions | Value | Insight |
|------|---------------------|-------|---------|
| 2018 | 100 million | ₹1 trillion | Nascent stage |

**India = 50%+ of global real-time digital payments** (more than US, UK, EU combined)

**Why UPI Won:**

1. **Zero MDR (Merchant Discount Rate)**: Free for merchants (unlike credit cards 1.5-3% fee) → Universal acceptance
2. **Instant settlement**: Money reaches seller in seconds (vs 2-3 days for cards)
3. **Interoperability**: Works across all banks, all apps (not locked to one wallet)
4. **Simple UX**: Just phone number or QR code (no typing 16-digit card numbers)
5. **24×7 availability**: Works on holidays, weekends, 3 AM
6. **Government push**: Demonetization (2016), Digital India mission

### Digital Safety Tips

**Common Scams (How to Avoid):**

| Scam Type | How It Works | Protection |
|-----------|--------------|------------|
| **Fake UPI IDs** | Scammer sends QR requesting money (looks like receiving) | ❌ NEVER scan QR to "receive" money (UPI pull scam) |
| **OTP Phishing** | Caller claims to be "bank executive," asks for OTP | ❌ NEVER share OTP (bank never calls asking for OTP) |
| **KYC Update Trap** | SMS/email says "KYC expired, click link" | ❌ Don't click links. Visit bank website directly |
| **APK Scam** | "Install this app for reward/refund" | ❌ Only install apps from Play Store/App Store |
| **Screen Share Fraud** | "Share screen to fix issue" (TeamViewer/AnyDesk) | ❌ Banks never ask to share screen |

**Golden Rules:**
1. **Bank never calls** asking for card number, CVV, PIN, OTP, password
2. **You cannot receive money** by scanning QR or clicking link (these are payment requests)
3. **Check UPI ID** before sending (scammers use names like "axisbank@paytm"—not real bank)
4. **Enable transaction SMS alerts** (₹25/month = best security investment)
5. **Use bank's official app** (don't click links in SMS/email)

## FAQs

**Q1: Why do PSU banks offer lower FD interest rates than small finance banks?**

**Answer**: **Demand-supply + trust arbitrage**.

**PSU banks (SBI, PNB, BoB)**:
- **Don't need to compete aggressively** on rates (already have ₹100+ lakh crore deposits due to govt salaries, pensions auto-credited)
- **Sovereign backing** (implicit government guarantee) = Depositors accept 0.5-1% lower rates for safety
- **Large existing customer base** (inertia—people don't switch banks for 0.5% more interest)

**Small finance banks (AU, Ujjivan, Equitas)**:
- **Newer**, must offer higher rates (6.5-7.5%) to attract deposits
- **Smaller trust factor** (DICGC insurance ₹5L limit protects most depositors, but perception lags)
- **Need to grow deposit base** to fund lending (can't lend without deposits)

**Risk-adjusted returns**:
- SBI FD 6.8% + perceived zero risk = Good for ₹10L+ (above DICGC limit)
- AU Bank FD 7.5% + DICGC protected (₹5L) = Good for amounts within insurance limit

**Our take**: Split large amounts—₹5L each in 2-3 banks (maximize insurance + interest)

---

**Q2: Is it safe to keep more than ₹5 lakh in one bank (DICGC insurance limit)?**

**Answer**: **Low risk with PSU banks, moderate risk with private/small finance banks.**

**DICGC (Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation)**:
- Insures **up to ₹5 lakh per depositor per bank** (principal + interest combined)
- Covers: Savings, FD, RD, current accounts (combined limit ₹5L)
- Activated if: Bank fails, RBI cancels license (rare but happens—PMC Bank 2019, Yes Bank 2020)

**Historical bank failures (2015-2024)**:
- **PSU banks**: Zero failures (government always bails out—SBI merged weak PSU banks to prevent collapse)
- **Private banks**: Yes Bank crisis (2020)—₹50,000 Cr withdrawal limit imposed, depositors got money after 6 months (no loss but liquidity freeze)
- **Cooperative banks**: 50+ failures (PMC Bank, CKP Co-op)—depositors waited 2-4 years for ₹5L insurance payout

**Strategy for ₹20L+ savings**:
- **Option 1**: ₹5L in 4 different banks (maximize insurance, diversify)
- **Option 2**: ₹10L in SBI/HDFC (PSU/large private bank, very low failure risk) + ₹5L in AU/IDFC (higher interest, within insurance)
- **Option 3**: ₹15L in bank FDs + ₹5L in debt mutual funds (liquid/ultra-short, higher returns ~6-7%, NAV risk but professional management)

**Q3: Should I close my old savings account (unused for years) or keep it?**

**Answer**: **Close if minimum balance penalty applies; keep if zero-balance.**

**Reasons to close:**
- **Minimum balance violation fees**: ₹600-750/month (₹7,200-9,000/year drain)
- **Dormant account hassles**: Unused for 2+ years = classified dormant (need branch visit to reactivate)
- **Security risk**: Old unused accounts = forgotten, hackers exploit (SIM swap fraud)
- **Simplify finances**: Easier to track 1-2 accounts vs 5-6

**Reasons to keep:**
- **Zero balance** (salary accounts converted to zero-balance) = No cost
- **Credit history**: Longer bank relationship = better loan approvals (HDFC gives preference to 5+ year customers)
- **Backup**: If primary bank system down, secondary account for emergencies

**How to close**:
1. Withdraw all money (leave ₹0 balance)
2. Visit branch (bring ID, debit card, cheque book)
3. Fill account closure form
4. Surrender debit card + unused cheques
5. Get written confirmation (closure letter)

**Dormant account risk**: Not closed but unused → RBI mandates banks freeze after 2 years (cannot transact online, must visit branch to prove identity)

---

**Q4: Why do banks offer pre-approved loans to some customers and not others?**

**Answer**: **Credit behavior scoring + relationship value.**

**Banks assess:**

**1. Salary credit pattern** (stable income):
- ₹50,000+ monthly salary for 12+ months = Pre-approved personal loan eligibility

**2. Credit card usage** (payment discipline):
- Pay on time for 6+ months + utilize 30-50% of limit (shows usage but not desperation) = Pre-approved limit increase

**3. Account balance** (surplus liquidity):
- Maintain ₹2L+ average balance = Signals financial stability

**4. Loan repayment history**:
- Existing home loan/car loan with bank, zero delays = Top priority for cross-sell

**5. Transaction patterns**:
- Spend on debit/credit card, bill payments, investments = "Active customer" (worth retaining)

**Why pre-approved matters**:
- **Interest rate**: 0.25-0.5% lower (10.5% vs 11%)
- **Processing time**: 48 hours vs 7-14 days
- **Documentation**: Minimal (salary slip + bank statement, both already with bank)

**How to increase chances**:
- Maintain salary account >1 year
- Use bank's credit card, pay on time
- Keep average balance ₹50,000+
- Avoid closing FDs prematurely (signals stability)

---

**Q5: What is the difference between NEFT, RTGS, IMPS, and UPI?**

**Answer**: **Transfer modes with different limits, speeds, and costs (but now mostly free + instant).**

| Feature | NEFT | RTGS | IMPS | UPI |
|---------|------|------|------|-----|
| **Full Name** | National Electronic Funds Transfer | Real Time Gross Settlement | Immediate Payment Service | Unified Payments Interface |
| **Speed** | 30 min - 2 hours (batch processing) | Instant (real-time) | Instant | Instant (2-5 sec) |
| **Timing** | 24×7 (since Dec 2019) | 24×7 (since Dec 2020) | 24×7 | 24×7 |
| **Min/Max Limit** | ₹1 - No limit | ₹2 - No limit | ₹1 - ₹5 lakh | ₹1 - ₹1 lakh |
| **Cost** | Free (since 2019) | Free (since 2019) | ₹2-5 (some banks) | Free |
| **Use Case** | Large transfers >₹1L (no UPI limit issue) | Very large (₹10L+, instant) | Urgent <₹5L | Daily transactions <₹1L |

**When to use what:**

- **₹500 to friend**: UPI (instant, free, just phone number)
- **₹50,000 credit card payment**: UPI or NEFT (both free)
- **₹2 lakh home down payment**: NEFT or RTGS (UPI ₹1L limit restricts)
- **₹10 lakh property purchase (same day)**: RTGS (instant settlement, large amount)

**RTGS shutdown** (pre-2020): Used to be 9 AM - 4:30 PM only (now 24×7, game-changer for emergencies)

**Q6: Can I have FDs in multiple banks to maximize DICGC insurance (above ₹5 lakh)?**

**Answer**: **Yes, legitimate strategy for ₹10L+ FD amounts.**

**DICGC insurance applies per depositor per bank** (not per account).

**Example (₹20L to invest in FDs)**:

**Single bank (risky)**:
- ₹20L FD in HDFC Bank
- **Insurance coverage**: ₹5 lakh only
- **At risk**: ₹15 lakh (if bank fails—unlikely but possible, see Yes Bank 2020)

**Split strategy (safer)**:
- ₹5L FD in HDFC Bank (fully insured)
- ₹5L FD in ICICI Bank (fully insured)
- ₹5L FD in Axis Bank (fully insured)
- ₹5L FD in SBI (fully insured)
- **Total insurance coverage**: ₹20 lakh (100% protected)

**Additional benefit**: Rate shopping (pick best FD rates from each bank for your chosen tenure)

**Trade-off**: Tracking hassle (4 maturity dates, 4 TDS certificates, 4 Form 26AS entries)

**Easier alternative for ₹20L+**: Debt mutual funds (liquid/ultra-short/banking & PSU funds)—no insurance needed (diversified across 30-50 bonds), higher post-tax returns (~6-7%), better liquidity

---

**Q7: Why is UPI free for users and merchants? How do banks/UPI apps make money?**

**Answer**: **Subsidized by government (NPCI) + Long-term business model (cross-sell financial products).**

**Cost structure**:

**Per UPI transaction cost to bank/NPCI**:
- Infrastructure: ₹0.50-1.00 (servers, security, settlement)
- **Who pays**: Currently **shared by banks + government subsidies**

**Zero MDR (Merchant Discount Rate)**:
- RBI mandate (since Jan 2020): **No charges for UPI/RuPay** transactions
- **Why**: Promote digital payments, reduce cash economy (tax collection improves)

**How UPI apps (PhonePe, GPay, Paytm) make money:**

| Revenue Source | Example | Annual Revenue |
|----------------|---------|----------------|
| **Financial product distribution** | Mutual funds (0.5% commission), insurance (10-30%), loans (1-2% referral) | ₹500-1,000 Cr (PhonePe) |
| **Merchant payments** | Large merchants pay for POS devices, QR, analytics | ₹100-200 Cr |
| **Advertising** | Promoted search, brand campaigns on app | ₹50-100 Cr |
| **Wealth management** | Paytm Money, PhonePe Wealth (brokerage, AMC commissions) | Growing |

**PhonePe/GPay strategy**: Lose money on UPI (₹2,000 Cr annual losses) BUT acquire 400M+ users → Cross-sell insurance (₹5,000 Cr premiums) + Mutual funds (₹20,000 Cr AUM) + Loans (₹50,000 Cr disbursals) = Profitable ecosystem

**Government benefit**: Every ₹100 UPI transaction (vs ₹100 cash) = ₹18 GST trackable + ₹5 income tax compliance improvement = ₹20B+ annual tax revenue gain (subsidizing UPI worth it)

## Conclusion: Your Banking Action Plan

**If you're starting fresh (first bank account):**

1. **Open salary account** with employer's tied-up bank (usually HDFC/ICICI/Axis—zero balance, pre-approved credit card)
2. **Enable UPI** on GPay/PhonePe (link salary account)
3. **Build emergency fund**: Save 3-6 months' expenses in **savings account** (₹1.5-3L for ₹30-50k salary)
4. **Start SIP**: ₹5,000-10,000/month in Nifty 50 index fund (long-term wealth, beats FD)
5. **Get credit card**: From salary account bank after 3 months (build credit history, pay on time)

---

**If you have ₹5L+ savings lying in low-interest savings account:**

1. **Reallocate**: Keep only 3-6 months expenses in savings (liquidity), move rest to:
   - ₹1-2L: Liquid mutual fund (7% returns, redeem in 24 hours vs FD locked)
   - ₹1-2L: 1-year FD (7% guaranteed, beats savings 3%)
   - ₹1-2L: Equity index fund SIP (12-15% long-term for 5+ year goals)
2. **Rate arbitrage**: Move surplus savings to IDFC First/AU Small Finance (4-7% vs 3% = ₹2,000-4,000 extra annual interest on ₹1L)

---

**If you're 50+ (retirement planning):**

1. **Preserve capital**: 60-70% in FDs (SBI/Post Office 7-7.5%) + Senior Citizen Savings Scheme (8.2%)
2. **Liquidity**: 10-15% in savings account (medical emergencies)
3. **Moderate growth**: 20-30% in balanced mutual funds (60% debt + 40% equity for inflation protection)
4. **Avoid**: High-risk stocks, crypto, long lock-in products (liquidity crucial at this age)
